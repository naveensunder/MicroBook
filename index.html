This course develops the analytical foundations of microeconomic theory, focusing on the behaviour of individual consumers and firms. Topics include consumer preferences and utility maximisation, demand theory and comparative statics, production and cost analysis, and market structures ranging from perfect competition to monopoly and oligopoly. Students will apply these tools to real-world markets, pricing strategies, and policy questions. A working knowledge of calculus is assumed; formal proofs are balanced with applied examples drawn from business and public policy.
